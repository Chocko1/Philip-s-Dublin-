Sutton

"If you see my Mother, will you tell her I am doing fine".

Philip Lynott purchased his home, Gleen Corr, in Sutton, Dublin, in the mid-1970s, cementing his ties to the picturesque coastal suburb. This period coincided with Lynott's rising prominence as a musician and songwriter, with Thin Lizzy achieving international success. Sutton provided Lynott with a serene backdrop for his creative endeavors, offering respite from the demands of his burgeoning career. Gleen Corr, nestled near the iconic Corr Castle, became synonymous with Lynott's legacy, serving as a hub for musical innovation and camaraderie. Its significance as the gatehouse to Corr Castle adds to its historical allure.
